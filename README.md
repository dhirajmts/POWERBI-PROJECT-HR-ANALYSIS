
# HR Analysis

## Problem Statement:

Organizations often struggle to effectively monitor employee performance, engagement, and attrition risk due to large volumes of HR data spread across multiple dimensions such as job role, department, experience, salary, and performance ratings.

The HR department needs a centralized, visual, and interactive dashboard to analyze employee performance trends, identify high and low performers, and understand the factors influencing productivity, satisfaction, and attrition.

This Power BI dashboard aims to transform raw HR data into meaningful insights that help management make data-driven decisions related to talent retention, performance improvement, workforce planning, and employee engagement.

### Objectives of the Dashboard:

⦁	Analyze employee performance ratings across departments and job roles  
⦁	Identify factors affecting high and low performance  
⦁	Monitor attrition trends and potential risk areas  
⦁	Understand the relationship between salary, experience, and performance  
⦁	Support HR leadership in strategic workforce decisions

### Dataset Overview:

⦁	Employee-level HR data  
⦁	Approx. 10,000 employee records

⦁	Includes:

    Demographics (Age, Gender, Education)
    Job details (Department, Role, Experience, Years at Company)
    Performance metrics (Performance Rating, Promotions)
    Compensation (Salary, Salary Hike)
    Attrition & Satisfaction indicators


### Important Visuals Used:

- Stacked Bar Chart: Performance Rating by Department  
- Pie Chart: Department vs Average Performance rating  
- Donut Chart: Education and Training hours  
- Area Chart: Attendance percentage by Gender  
- Line Chart: Job role vs Performance Rating vs Education  
- Ribbon Chart: Attendance percentage vs Department vs Education  
- Slicers: Department, Employee ID  
- Cards

### Conclusion:

The HR Analytics Power BI dashboard provides actionable insights into employee performance and workforce behavior, enabling HR leaders to move from reactive decision-making to a proactive, data-driven approach.
